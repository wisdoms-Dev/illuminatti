Philosophy

Six Tenets, Held Together

These are not steps to complete, but commitments held at once — each reinforcing the rest. (A fictional philosophy, written for this story.)

Lifelong Learning

The Council treats curiosity as a discipline in itself — study is never finished, only deepened.

Self-Discipline

Mastery of one's own habits is regarded as the foundation every other pursuit is built upon.

Leadership

To lead is to serve first — initiators are asked to earn trust before they ask for it.

Wisdom

Knowledge is common; wisdom is knowing when, and when not, to act on it.

Service to Humanity

Personal advancement is held to matter only insofar as it is eventually given back.

Personal Achievement

Excellence is pursued not for status, but as evidence that the other tenets were kept.
